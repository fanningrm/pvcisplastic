Fact Checking PVC

The PVC industry habitually spreads mistruths to its customers and the public. We're providing this resource to expose those lies, and make sure that the truth prevails.

  • FALSE: PVC lasts in excess of 110 years, according to the American Water Works Association. A European study determined its longevity at 170 years. (Source)
  • TRUE: The service life for modern Ductile Iron Pipe is expected to be at least 105 years. PVC’s service life is as low as 55 years. (Source)
  • FALSE: PVC pipe is a completely recyclable...alternative to traditional corrosion-prone piping materials. (Source)
  • FALSE: PVC pipe is one of the safest and most tested materials used in North America. (Source)
  • FALSE: The evidence shows it [PVC] neither leaches chemicals like lead, cadmium, BPA or plasticizers, nor does it release harmful organotins, nor pose major hazards in its manufacture, use and disposal, nor create a dangerous bio-film, nor form dioxins as water passes through, etc. (Source)
  • FALSE: PVC pipe is the industry standard: less energy and fewer resources are needed to manufacture and install it. (Source)
  • FALSE: One of the world’s most durable products with arguably no known end-life, PVC is the most sustainable piping material available. (Source)
  • FALSE: PVC has a lower carbon footprint than both ductile iron and recycled ductile iron pipe. (Source)
  • FALSE: There are almost no valid technical reasons for not including PVC pipe in municipal bids except when operating pressures are above 305 psi or when water temperature is above 140°F. (Source)
  • FALSE: PVC pipe manufacturing is extremely efficient, with virtually 100 percent of the PVC compound being used. It takes four times less energy to make than concrete pipe, and half that used for iron pipe. (Source)
  • FALSE: Up to 70 percent less expensive than iron pipe, PVC pipe is an easy winner when it comes to saving water utilities money on capital expenditures. (Source)
  • TRUE: The increased flow capacity of Ductile Iron Pipe leads to lower pumping costs and significant energy savings. In fact, pumping through Ductile Iron Pipe instead of PVC can result in as much as a 38% savings in energy consumption. These energy savings result in less CO2 production, thereby reducing greenhouse gas emissions into the environment. (Source)
  • FALSE: Iron Piping is not "Green". (Source)
  • TRUE: Ductile Iron Pipe is a 100 % recyclable material. It presents no adverse health risks. (Source)
  • TRUE: Over seven billion pounds of PVC are thrown away each year in the U.S., and only a fraction of that is recycled – less than .50% (Source)
  • FALSE: PVC pipe’s greatest environmental attribute is its exceptional durability and corrosion resistance – leading to better water conservation and lower replacement, maintenance and repair costs. (Source)
  • FALSE: PVC pipe has the lowest overall failure rate when compared to cast iron, ductile iron, concrete, steel and asbestos cement pipes. (Source)
  • TRUE: Ductile Iron Pipe is not prone to permeation and the contamination of drinking water that may result, whereas plastic pipe is susceptible to permeation that can damage the pipe and contaminate the water. In fact, in a study prepared for the EPA, plastic pipe was involved in 98% of permeation incidents. (Source)
