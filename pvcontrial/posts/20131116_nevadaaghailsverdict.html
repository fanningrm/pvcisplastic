Nevada Appeal: Nevada AG hails verdict in defective PVC pipe case

Nevada Attorney General Catherine Cortez Masto is hailing a jury verdict that found a company knowingly made defective water and sewer pipes sold to dozens of municipalities and water districts across several states.

Posted by PVC is Plastic on November 16, 2013

Nevada Attorney General Catherine Cortez Masto is hailing a jury verdict that found a company knowingly made defective water and sewer pipes sold to dozens of municipalities and water districts across several states.

Thursday’s verdict in U.S. District Court in Los Angeles against JM Manufacturing capped a seven-year legal case brought by Nevada, New Mexico, Virginia and 42 cities and water districts.

“It’s been a hard fought victory,” Masto said in a statement. “We know from firsthand experience that this PVC pipe will prematurely lead or break and can jeopardize lives.”

The verdict, she said, “demonstrates that manufacturers cannot get away with fraud that puts lives at risk.”
